Research Overview

Traditional market microstructure models rely on matrix factorization methods like PCA, which flatten multi-dimensional market data and lose critical interaction effects. This research demonstrates that cryptocurrency markets exhibit low-dimensional tensor structure induced by microstructure dynamics, and that tensor decomposition methods preserve these multi-way interactions better than traditional approaches.

üéØ Core Thesis

Cryptocurrency markets do not live on flat planes‚Äîthey evolve on curved, low-dimensional manifolds induced by microstructure dynamics. Tensor methods respect this geometry; matrix methods erase it through flattening.

Key Contributions

  • Novel Framework: First application of tensor decomposition to cryptocurrency market microstructure with systematic comparison to matrix baselines
  • Empirical Validation: Analysis of 8,761 hours of OHLCV data across BTC, ETH, and SOL from Binance
  • Methodological Insights: Demonstration that low-rank tensor structure exists in cryptocurrency markets and can be exploited for dimensionality reduction
  • Economic Interpretation: Extraction of four interpretable market factors corresponding to regime dynamics and cross-asset correlations

Economic Interpretation of Factors

Tensor decomposition extracts four economically meaningful factors from cryptocurrency market microstructure:

Factor 1: Bitcoin Dominance & Systematic Risk

Primary factor capturing BTC's role as "digital gold" and systematic risk driver across the cryptocurrency ecosystem. High loadings during market-wide movements.

Factor 2: Altcoin Rotation & Risk Appetite

Second factor representing ETH-SOL dynamics and altcoin rotation patterns. Reflects shifts in risk appetite and speculation cycles.

Factor 3: Volatility Regimes & Liquidity Shocks

Third factor corresponding to regime transitions between high/low volatility states and liquidity shock events.

Factor 4: Intraday Microstructure & Mean Reversion

Fourth factor capturing intraday patterns, mean reversion dynamics, and hourly microstructure effects.
